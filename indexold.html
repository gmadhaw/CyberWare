// Breach Intelligence

Real Breaches.
Real Lessons.

Analysed by industry sector. Every incident is a case study in what can go wrong — and how to prevent it.

Marks & Spencer (2025)
April 2025 £300M+ estimated impact Ransomware

Scattered Spider threat group compromised M&S via a third-party IT provider, encrypting systems and exfiltrating customer data. Online orders halted for weeks, contactless payments disrupted across 1,000+ stores.

🔐 Key Lesson Third-party and supply chain risk management is non-negotiable. MFA bypass via social engineering of IT helpdesk was the entry vector.
Target Corporation (2013)
Dec 2013 40M cards stolen POS Malware

Attackers entered via a third-party HVAC vendor's credentials, deployed RAM-scraping malware across POS terminals, capturing 40M credit/debit card details and 70M personal records over 3 weeks.

🔐 Key Lesson Vendor network segmentation prevents lateral movement. Privileged access should never reach payment systems.
Co-op Group (2025)
May 2025 20M+ members affected Data Exfiltration

DragonForce ransomware group exfiltrated member data including names and contact details. Attack followed similar pattern to the M&S breach, suggesting coordinated campaign targeting UK retail.

🔐 Key Lesson Sector-wide threat intelligence sharing is critical. When one retailer is hit, peer organisations should immediately review their exposure.
Equifax (2017)
Jul–Sep 2017 147M records · $575M settlement Unpatched Vuln

An unpatched Apache Struts vulnerability (CVE-2017-5638) sat exposed for 78 days. Attackers exfiltrated SSNs, birth dates, and financial data. A known patch had been available for months before exploitation.

🔐 Key Lesson Patch management SLAs must be enforced. Critical vulnerabilities require patching within 24–72 hours, not weeks.
Santander Bank (2024)
May 2024 30M customers & staff Supply Chain

A third-party database provider was compromised, exposing data for customers in Spain, Chile, Uruguay plus all current and some former employees. ShinyHunters claimed responsibility.

🔐 Key Lesson Data minimisation at third parties and regular supplier security assessments are essential financial sector controls.
Change Healthcare (2024)
Feb 2024 $22B+ disruption · 100M+ patients Ransomware

ALPHV/BlackCat ransomware crippled the largest US health payment processor. A Citrix portal without MFA was the entry point. Pharmacies, hospitals, and insurers lost transaction capability for weeks.

🔐 Key Lesson MFA on every external-facing system, no exceptions. Business continuity planning must account for critical payment infrastructure failure.
British Library (2023)
Oct 2023 600GB stolen · £7M recovery Ransomware

Rhysida ransomware group exfiltrated 600GB including staff HR data, user information, and internal files. Data auctioned on dark web for 20 Bitcoin. Systems remained offline for months.

🔐 Key Lesson Public institutions often under-invest in cyber resilience. Offline backups and incident response plans must be tested, not just documented.
Synnovis / NHS (2024)
Jun 2024 1,134 operations postponed Ransomware

Qilin ransomware attacked Synnovis (NHS blood testing partner), dumping 400GB of patient data publicly. Over 1,000 operations and appointments were cancelled or postponed across London hospitals.

🔐 Key Lesson Healthcare supply chain resilience is a patient safety issue. Critical NHS partners require the same security standards as NHS trusts themselves.
Ministry of Defence (2024)
May 2024 272,000 personnel records Third-Party Breach

A payroll system operated by SSCL (Shared Services Connected Ltd) was compromised. Names and bank details of 272,000 current and former military personnel were exposed. Suspected nation-state actor.

🔐 Key Lesson Government outsourcing contracts must include enforceable security standards. Sensitive data in third-party systems requires continuous monitoring.
Colonial Pipeline (2021)
May 2021 $4.4M ransom · Fuel shortage Ransomware

DarkSide ransomware encrypted Colonial's IT network. Out of caution, the company shut down OT pipeline operations, causing fuel shortages across the US East Coast. Entry via a compromised VPN password from a leaked credential database.

🔐 Key Lesson IT/OT network segmentation is critical. A leaked password without MFA should never be able to threaten physical infrastructure.
Ukraine Power Grid (2015–16)
Dec 2015 & 2016 230,000 customers without power Nation-State ICS Attack

Sandworm (Russia GRU) used BlackEnergy malware and spear-phishing to compromise ICS/SCADA systems. Attackers manually opened breakers and wiped firmware on serial-to-Ethernet converters to prevent recovery.

🔐 Key Lesson ICS systems require air-gapping or strict network segmentation. Human-machine interface access must be tightly controlled and monitored.
Oldsmar Water Treatment (2021)
Feb 2021 NaOH levels raised 111x Remote Access Compromise

An attacker used TeamViewer remote access software to increase sodium hydroxide levels in a Florida water treatment plant to potentially lethal concentrations. An operator noticed the mouse moving and intervened.

🔐 Key Lesson Remote access to OT/ICS environments must use privileged access workstations, MFA, and session recording. Consumer remote tools have no place in critical infrastructure.
23andMe (2023)
Oct 2023 6.9M genetic profiles Credential Stuffing

Attackers used credential stuffing to access 14,000 accounts, then leveraged the DNA Relatives feature to scrape 6.9M connected profiles. Genetic, ancestry, and health predisposition data was exposed and sold.

🔐 Key Lesson Genetic data is the most sensitive PII. Opt-in data sharing features require step-up authentication. Password reuse by users is the attacker's cheapest weapon.
NHS Dumfries & Galloway (2024)
Mar 2024 3TB patient data dumped Ransomware

INC Ransom published 3 terabytes of sensitive patient data after the Scottish NHS board refused to pay. Clinical records, referrals, and personal identifiable data of tens of thousands were exposed publicly.

🔐 Key Lesson Paying ransom does not guarantee data deletion. Data exfiltration prevention (DLP) controls must be the priority alongside endpoint protection.
HCRG Care Group (2025)
Feb 2025 2.03TB patient records Ransomware

Ransomhub ransomware group exfiltrated over 2TB of sensitive data including patient records from the NHS community health provider. Employee passport scans and personal data were among the stolen files.

🔐 Key Lesson Community healthcare providers are high-value, low-defence targets. Investment in cyber resilience must match the sensitivity of the data held.
